Recommended Exercises for Heart Disease Stages

Exercise for Stage 1: Early/Asymptomatic Stage

Walking

Take a brisk walk for 20â€“30 minutes daily. It helps control weight, blood pressure, and cholesterol while strengthening your heart.

Exercise for Stage 2: Angina (Chest Pain) Stage

Chair Exercises

Perform seated leg lifts, arm raises, or light stretches. These exercises improve circulation with minimal strain on the heart.

Exercise for Stage 3: Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction) Stage

Cardiac Rehabilitation

Join a supervised rehab program with tailored exercises like treadmill walking and stationary cycling under medical guidance.

Exercise for Stage 4: Heart Failure

Low-Impact Aerobics

Light movements such as slow dancing or water aerobics improve circulation and breathing without overworking the heart.

Exercise for Stage 5: End-Stage Heart Disease

Assisted Stretching & Breathing

Simple stretches and deep-breathing exercises, ideally with caregiver support, help ease discomfort and promote gentle mobility.
