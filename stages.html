Stages of Heart Disease

Stage 1: Early/Asymptomatic Stage

Heart disease is silently developing with no noticeable symptoms. Risk factors like hypertension or high cholesterol are present.

  • No visible symptoms
  • High blood pressure or cholesterol
  • Lifestyle risks: smoking, poor diet, inactivity

Stage 2: Angina (Chest Pain) Stage

Blood flow to the heart is partially blocked, causing chest pain during physical or emotional stress.

  • Chest pain on exertion
  • Radiating pain (arms, neck, jaw)
  • Shortness of breath, tiredness

Stage 3: Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)

A heart attack occurs when blood flow is completely blocked, damaging the heart muscle.

  • Severe chest pain or pressure
  • Nausea, sweating, dizziness
  • Pain lasting more than 20 minutes

Stage 5: End-Stage Heart Disease

Severe damage has occurred. The heart may no longer function even with medication. Often requires transplant or palliative care.

  • Severe fatigue and breathlessness
  • Recurrent hospitalizations
  • Dependence on oxygen or devices
